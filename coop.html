Co-op Experiences

Most of my co-op experience is in the Project Management space relating to Civil Infrastructure, but I am open to any type of experiences moving forward, including experiences in consulting, software, and infrasturucture design.

First Co-op: WSP

During my time at WSP, I was able to work on a wide range of projects. I was in a client facing role, which allowed me to develop my communication skills as well as learn how to interact with clients and deal with any issues that arose.

My range of projects included retail infrastructure, land development and trail works projects. I was assigned to help manage over 10 projects at a time with my team which improved my time management skills.

The most enjoyable part of this term is when I was given the opportunity to advise clients on the financial side of projects. I was able to advise clients on potential partnerships and do this by developing excel spreadsheets with analysis I had done on project financing.

My 10 main takeaways from this co-op term are as follows:
1. Contracts are never executed the way they're drawn up. Things are different on paper than in real life. Consulting firms need to be ready to make adjustments to help the project go smoothly for the client.
